A street-cart-style chicken and rice plate with spiced rice, crisp salad, and white sauce.

## Ingredients

### Marinade
- [ ] 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- [ ] 3 cloves garlic
- [ ] 1 tablespoon dried oregano
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
- [ ] 1/4 cup olive oil
- [ ] 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon MSG (optional, but recommended)
- [ ] 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper

### Chicken
- [ ] 1 tablespoon olive oil
- [ ] 2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs

### Rice
- [ ] 1 1/2 cups long-grain or basmati rice, rinsed and drained
- [ ] 1 tablespoon butter
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon ground cumin
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
- [ ] 2 1/2 cups chicken broth
- [ ] Salt and pepper, to taste
- [ ] Pinch MSG (optional)

### White sauce
- [ ] 1/2 cup mayonnaise
- [ ] 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
- [ ] 1 tablespoon sugar
- [ ] 2 tablespoons distilled white vinegar
- [ ] 1 teaspoon lemon juice
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- [ ] 1/4 teaspoon MSG (optional)
- [ ] 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- [ ] 2 tablespoons chopped parsley or dill

### For serving
- [ ] Shredded iceberg lettuce
- [ ] Chopped tomatoes
- [ ] Hot sauce
- [ ] Thinly sliced onion, soaked in warm water 5 to 10 minutes (optional)
- [ ] Pita bread (optional)

## Instructions

1. Make the marinade by blending the lemon juice, garlic, oregano, coriander, 1/4 cup olive oil, salt, MSG, and black pepper until roughly emulsified.
2. Put the chicken in a bowl and pour over half the marinade, reserving the rest. Massage to coat well, then cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour and up to 4 hours. Do not marinate much longer or the chicken can turn dry and chalky. If needed, you can cook it right away.
3. Heat 1 tablespoon olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken in a single layer and cook until browned on the first side, about 5 minutes. Flip and cook until browned and at least 74°C / 165°F inside, about 5 minutes more. Lower the heat if the pan starts to scorch. Transfer the chicken to a plate and keep the pan with its fat and browned bits.
4. For the rice, add the butter to the same skillet over medium heat. Stir in the cumin and turmeric for about 30 seconds, then add the rice and cook for 1 minute, stirring, until glossy. Add the chicken broth and season lightly with salt, pepper, and MSG. Bring to a boil, reduce to a bare simmer, cover, and cook for 15 minutes. Turn off the heat and let it sit, covered, for 10 more minutes.
5. Meanwhile, make the white sauce by whisking together the mayonnaise, yogurt, sugar, vinegar, lemon juice, salt, MSG, black pepper, and parsley or dill. Taste and adjust. It should be tangy, lightly sweet, and a little salty.
6. Chop the cooked chicken into small bite-size pieces. Toss with the reserved marinade and any juices from the plate.
7. Fluff the rice and transfer it to a serving platter or bowl.
8. Return the chopped chicken and marinade to the skillet over medium-high heat. Cook, stirring constantly, until the marinade reduces and coats the meat, a few minutes. Taste and adjust with salt, pepper, MSG, and lemon juice if needed.
9. Spoon the chicken over the rice and serve with lettuce, tomatoes, white sauce, hot sauce, onion, and pita if using.
