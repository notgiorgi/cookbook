# Spicy Beef Lo Mein

A spicy, savory lo mein with marinated beef and chilies.

## Yield
2 to 3 servings

## Time
35 minutes

## Ingredients

### Noodles
- 200 g lo mein noodles, fresh or par-cooked
- Neutral oil, as needed for stir-frying

### Marinated Beef
- About 200 g beef sirloin, thinly sliced
- 10 g light soy sauce
- 3 g sugar
- 2 g baking soda
- 10 g cornstarch
- 10 g chili crisp oil

### Sauce
- 50 g oyster sauce
- 10 g light soy sauce
- 10 g dark soy sauce
- 5 g beef bouillon paste

### Aromatics and Vegetables
- 1 shallot, diced
- Thai chilies, sliced, to taste
- 1/2 bell pepper, sliced
- 1 carrot, julienned
- 1 handful mushrooms, sliced
- Scallions, chopped, for garnish

## Instructions
1. Boil the noodles to the low end of the package instructions so they stay slightly underdone. Drain and set aside.
2. Slice the beef thinly against the grain into bite-sized pieces.
3. Mix the beef with soy sauce, sugar, baking soda, chili crisp, and cornstarch until evenly coated.
4. Stir together the sauce ingredients.
5. Heat oil in a wok over high heat and sear the beef in a single layer until browned and just cooked through. Work in batches if needed. Remove and set aside.
6. Add more oil if needed and stir-fry the shallot and chilies until fragrant.
7. Add the carrots, bell pepper, and mushrooms and cook until softened.
8. Add the noodles and sauce and toss until glossy and evenly coated.
9. Return the beef to the wok and toss to combine.
10. Finish with scallions.
