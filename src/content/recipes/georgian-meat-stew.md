A tomato-forward Georgian-style meat stew built from browned neck meat, caramelized onions, and fresh herbs at the end.

## Ingredients

- [ ] 1 kg meat from the neck cut, cut into stew pieces
- [ ] Neutral oil, as needed
- [ ] 1 kg fresh tomatoes, blanched, peeled, and chopped, or an equivalent canned tomato and passata mix
- [ ] 400 g onions, sliced or chopped
- [ ] 1 tablespoon ground coriander
- [ ] 1 tablespoon fenugreek or blue fenugreek
- [ ] Salt, to taste
- [ ] 2 to 3 bay leaves
- [ ] 12 g cilantro, chopped
- [ ] 18 g parsley, chopped

## Instructions

1. Blanch the tomatoes, peel them, and chop them. If using canned tomatoes and passata instead, combine them now.
2. Add some oil to a heavy pot, then add the tomatoes. Set the pot over low to medium heat and let them start cooking down.
3. While the tomatoes cook, brown the meat in a pan until well colored.
4. Save the rendered fat and use it to caramelize the onions.
5. After the tomatoes have simmered for about 20 minutes, add the browned meat and the caramelized onions to the pot.
6. Season with salt, ground coriander, fenugreek, and bay leaves.
7. Continue simmering gently for 30 to 40 minutes more, until the meat is tender and the stew is rich.
8. Chop the cilantro and parsley, stir them into the stew, then turn off the heat.

## Notes

- The source notes specify a 1 kg batch, 400 g onion, and a herb finish of 12 g cilantro plus 18 g parsley.
- The fenugreek and coriander were assumed as 1 tablespoon each, as requested.
- Fresh tomatoes were assumed as 1 kg, with canned tomato plus passata accepted as an equivalent shortcut.
