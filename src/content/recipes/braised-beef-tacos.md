A Georgia-friendly, milder version of the CookWell braised beef tacos, built with easier-to-find local ingredients and gentler heat.

## Ingredients

### Braised beef

- [ ] 1.1 to 1.4 kg beef chuck, shoulder, or another braising cut
- [ ] Salt, to taste
- [ ] Neutral oil, as needed for searing
- [ ] 30 g tomato paste
- [ ] 2 tablespoons sweet paprika
- [ ] 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- [ ] 1/4 to 1/2 teaspoon mild chili flakes or hot paprika, optional
- [ ] 15 g beef bouillon paste, or 1 beef stock cube
- [ ] 6 cloves garlic, smashed
- [ ] 1.5 liters water

### For serving

- [ ] Small flour tortillas, or thin lavash cut into taco-sized pieces
- [ ] 1 small onion, finely chopped
- [ ] A small handful of cilantro or parsley, chopped
- [ ] Lemon wedges
- [ ] Mild hot sauce or adjika, optional

## Instructions

1. Salt the beef and let it sit for 30 minutes at room temperature, or dry-brine it overnight in the fridge.
2. Heat a Dutch oven or heavy pot over high heat with a little oil. Sear the beef well on all sides until deeply browned, then remove it briefly.
3. In a bowl or jug, whisk together the water, tomato paste, paprika, cumin, optional chili, and bouillon until smooth.
4. Add the smashed garlic to the pot and cook for 1 to 2 minutes, just until fragrant.
5. Return the beef to the pot, pour in the braising liquid, and bring it to a simmer.
6. Cover and cook in a 135°C oven for 5 to 6 hours, until the beef is very tender and easy to shred.
7. Shred the beef and mix it back into the braising liquid.
8. Lightly dip the tortillas or lavash pieces in the braising liquid, then warm and lightly crisp them in a hot pan.
9. Fill with beef and top with chopped onion, herbs, and a squeeze of lemon. Add mild hot sauce or adjika if you want a bit more heat.

## Notes

- Sweet paprika replaces guajillo powder because it is easier to find locally and keeps the flavor milder.
- Lemon stands in for lime when limes are unavailable or expensive.
- Parsley works well if cilantro is hard to find or not wanted.
- Lavash is a practical fallback if good corn tortillas are not around.
