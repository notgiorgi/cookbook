# Chicken Tikka Masala

Chicken thigh pieces marinated in yogurt and spices, then simmered in a rich tomato-cream sauce.

## Ingredients

### Marinade
- 1 cup (240 g) full-fat yogurt
- 2 teaspoons (6 g) kosher salt
- 1.25 tablespoons (11 g) garam masala
- 1 teaspoon (3 g) paprika
- 5 cloves garlic, finely chopped
- 2-inch (5 cm) knob ginger, grated
- 2 pounds (907 g) boneless skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces

### Sauce
- 3 tablespoons (24 g) vegetable oil
- 1 large yellow onion, thinly sliced
- 2-inch (5 cm) knob ginger, grated or thinly sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 1 teaspoon (3 g) paprika
- 1 teaspoon (3 g) cumin powder
- 1/2 teaspoon turmeric
- 1 tablespoon (9 g) garam masala
- 1 tablespoon (24 g) unsalted butter
- 2 teaspoons (9 g) sugar
- 15 ounces (425 g) crushed tomatoes
- 1 cup (237 ml) heavy cream
- 1/3 cup fresh cilantro leaves
- Salt, to taste

## Instructions
1. Mix the yogurt, salt, garam masala, paprika, garlic, and ginger in a bowl.
2. Add the chicken and toss well. Marinate for 10 minutes or overnight in the refrigerator.
3. Heat oil in a wide pan over medium-high heat and sear the chicken in batches for 2 to 3 minutes per side, until browned.
4. Remove the chicken and add more oil if needed. Lower the heat to medium.
5. Cook the onion, ginger, and garlic with a little salt for 3 to 5 minutes, until softened.
6. Add the paprika, cumin, turmeric, garam masala, butter, and sugar, and cook for about 30 seconds.
7. Stir in the crushed tomatoes and cook for 5 minutes.
8. For a smoother sauce, blend and return it to the pan.
9. Add the chicken back in, lower the heat, and simmer for about 10 minutes, until cooked through and slightly reduced.
10. Stir in the heavy cream and simmer for 7 to 8 minutes more, until thick and velvety.
11. Adjust the salt and serve with rice, topped with cilantro if you like.
